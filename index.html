The Festival Queen

In the world of Uma Musume: Pretty Derby, Kitasan Black is known for her boundless energy, bright personality, and her deep love for traditional Japanese festivals (Matsuri).

Always looking up to her idol, Tokai Teio, Kitasan's journey is one of immense growth. Starting as a cheerful admirer, she develops into a true powerhouse on the turf, characterized by her incredible stamina and signature front-running tactics that leave the competition in the dust.

Whether she's leading a race or organizing a neighborhood festival, she brings a spirit of joy and perseverance that inspires everyone around her.

The Real Legend

The real-life Kitasan Black (foaled March 10, 2012) is one of the highest-earning Japanese racehorses in history. Owned by Ono Shoji (and closely associated with famous enka singer Saburo Kitajima), he became a massive fan favorite during his racing career.

Known for his robust health, incredible cruising speed, and resilience, he rarely missed a major race. After retiring in a blaze of glory by winning the Arima Kinen in 2017, he has gone on to become a highly successful sire. Most notably, he is the father of the phenomenal Equinox, who carried on his legacy by dominating the global racing scene.

Career Highlights

A two-time Japanese Horse of the Year (2016, 2017) and a 2020 inductee into the Japan Racing Association Hall of Fame.
